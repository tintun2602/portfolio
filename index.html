BI NORWEGIAN BUSINESS SCHOOL

The Master of Business Administration program is a broad and versatile education, which places great emphasis on analytical and strategic thinking. You need to like translating data, theory and past experiences into insights companies and organizations can use to adapt to an ever-changing world. The study takes today's challenges seriously, and takes into account that sustainability, profitability and innovation are closely linked. The program connects economic thinking with sustainability and global challenges. Knowledge of sustainability is relevant and in demand in the market, and an important element for understanding how today's global economy is connected. If you want to contribute to creating more sustainable businesses, sustainable business models are needed. By understanding economics, we can better understand which choices are both profitable for ourselves, different and the world. Sustainable value creation requires efficient resource utilization, innovation and the ability to adapt. The program has an international orientation and has contact with the business community throughout all five years. You will also learn about how international markers are evolving and how companies are meeting international demand.

Topics I learned about:

  • Finance
  • Macroeconomics
  • Microeconomics
  • Econometrics
  • Business law
  • Financial accounting
  • Strategy
  • Mathematics for economists
  • Statistics
